About Our Emblem

The school logo is the official visual identity of Siddhartha Secondary School. Every element — from the shield to the doves — has been deliberately chosen to represent the school's founding mission and its role in the community.

School Name: Siddhartha Secondary School

Established: 2051 B.S. (1994 A.D.)

Location: Sundarharaincha-10, Morang, Koshi Province

Abbreviation: S.S.S. — inscribed four times in the Star of David

Shape: Heraldic shield within a circular emblem

Motto Banner: Yellow-green ribbon bearing the school's address

{# COMPONENTS SECTION #}
Symbol Breakdown

Elements of the Logo Explained

{# 1. Outer Circle & Name #}

Outer Circle & School Name

A bold red circular ring frames the entire emblem. The school's full name — SIDDHARTHA SECONDARY SCHOOL — is written in large blue capital letters along the top arc, making the institution immediately recognisable.

Red Ring Blue Text
{# 2. Heraldic Shield #}

Heraldic Shield

At the heart of the logo sits a classic heraldic shield with a cream-yellow background, outlined in bold green. The shield is a universal symbol of protection, strength, and institutional pride — representing the school as a safe and nurturing environment for all students.

Green Border Cream Fill
{# 3. Star of David (Six-Pointed Star) #}

Six-Pointed Star

A prominent red six-pointed star (Star of David) dominates the shield. In the four outer triangular points of the star, the letters S S S S stand for Siddhartha Secondary School. The star symbolises enlightenment, direction, and the guiding light of knowledge shining in all directions.

Red Star
{# 4. Open Book #}

Open Book

Placed at the centre of the star, a cyan open book is the most direct symbol of the school's core purpose — education and learning. It represents the pursuit of knowledge, academic excellence, and the open minds the school aims to cultivate in every student.

Cyan / Light Blue
{# 5. Two Doves #}

Two Doves

On either side of the shield, two white doves are depicted in flight, outlined in cyan. Doves are a universal symbol of peace, freedom, and hope. Their presence flanking the shield signifies the school's commitment to nurturing a peaceful, harmonious learning community.

Cyan Outline
{# 6. Ribbon Banner #}

Ribbon Banner

A bold yellow-green ribbon unfurls beneath the entire emblem, bearing the school's address: Sundarharaincha-10, Morang in elegant script. The ribbon grounds the logo geographically, connecting the school to its local community and roots in Koshi Province.

Yellow-Green / Lime
